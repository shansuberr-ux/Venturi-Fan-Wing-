# Venturi-Profile Ceiling Fan Blade Design

> **Fluid Mechanics · CAD Design · Fan Aerodynamics · Product Design**

A ceiling fan redesign where each blade incorporates an internal Venturi-profile channel. As the blades rotate, a pressure differential across the blade cross-section passively drives air through an internal converging channel and expels it through underside outlet slots — adding a secondary jet component on top of the blade's primary downwash.

## The Concept

Standard ceiling fan blades are flat or slightly cambered — moving air purely through aerodynamic lift from rotation. This design adds a second mechanism: each blade has an internal converging channel drawing air in from upper-surface inlet slots (higher pressure) and expelling it through underside outlet slots (lower pressure) as the blade spins.

**Two simultaneous airflow contributions per blade:**
1. **Primary downwash** — conventional aerodynamic push from rotation
2. **Secondary jet** — internally channeled flow expelled through underside slots, augmenting downwash

## Engineering Principles

The pressure differential between upper and lower blade surfaces — the same differential that generates aerodynamic lift — drives airflow through the internal channel without any additional power input. The converging geometry accelerates this flow, increasing the jet exit velocity.

The value of this design is **more airflow per watt** (higher CFM/W efficiency), not lower air temperature. Perceived cooling from any fan is purely from increased airflow accelerating evaporative cooling on skin.

**What makes this novel:** internally ducted fan blades are well-studied in aerospace (blown flaps, active flow control) but are largely absent from consumer ceiling fan design. This applies those principles to a mundane product category.
